Krishnakumar Kunnath


Popularly known as KK, played an very important role as playback singer in Indian music industry.

About the Legend

☛ KK, in full Krishnakumar Kunnath was born on 23 August 1968 in Delhi, India.

☛ KK attended Delhi's Mount St Mary's School, and Kirori Mal College, Delhi University.

☛ After graduating in commerce from Kirori Mal College, KK did a six-month stint as a marketing executive before pursuing his passion for music. He struggled to establish himself in the competitive recording industry at first, resorting to singing at hotels to make ends meet.After a few years, he moved to Mumbai in 1994.

☛ He recorded songs in several languages, including Hindi, Tamil, Telugu, Kannada, Malayalam, Marathi, Odia, Bengali, Assamese and Gujarati.

☛ KK made his Bollywood debut with the song "Tadap Tadap Ke Is Dil Se" from Hum Dil De Chuke Sanam (1999).However, prior to this song, he had sung parts of Chhod Aaye Hum from Gulzar's Maachis (1996) He considered the song "Tadap Tadap Ke Is Dil Se" the turning point of his career.

☛ KK's popular songs included "Tadap Tadap Ke Is Dil Se" from Hum Dil De Chuke Sanam (1999), "Kya Mujhe Pyaar Hai" from Woh Lamhe... (2006), "Aankhon Mein Teri" from Om Shanti Om (2007), "Khuda Jane" from Bachna Ae Haseeno (2008), "Piya Aaye Na" from Aashiqui 2 (2013).

☛ KK received six Filmfare Awards nominations, but did not win any. In 2009, he won the Screen Awards, Best Playback Singer – Male, for his song " Khuda Jaane " from the film Bachna Ae Haseeno .

☛ In 1999, Sony Music had just been launched in India and was looking to launch a new artiste. KK was selected and released his debut solo album titled Pal with music composed by Lesle Lewis from the duo Colonial Cousins, who also arranged and produced the album

☛ KK sang many songs for television serials, such as Just Mohabbat, Shaka Laka Boom Boom, Kuch Jhuki Si Palkein, Hip Hip Hurray, Kkavyanjali and Just Dance.He appeared on television as a jury member for the talent hunt show Fame Gurukul.

☛ KK participated in the MTV India musical programme Coke Studio, in which he sang a qawwali, "Chadta Suraj", along with the Sabri Brothers, and a recomposed version of "Tu Aashiqui Hai" from the film Jhankaar Beats.

☛ On 29 August 2015, KK appeared in the television singing reality show Indian Idol Junior Season 2. He was featuring in a singing reality show as a judge and guest jury member after ten years.

☛ In 2019, in an interview to Hindustan Times KK said that he was active in the music industry through live performances and playback singing. The live performances in front of the audience made him happy and he did not want to break away from his "commitment to the audience". He had always wanted to release a new album after the successful 2008 release of his second.

☛ On 31 May 2022, KK performed a live music concert in the Gurudas College festival at Nazrul Mancha auditorium, in South Kolkata.After his performance on the way back to his hotel, KK collapsed and died from an cardiac arrest.He was 53 years old at the time of his death.

☛ In 2022, KK worked with film maker Srijit Mukherji and veteran lyricist Gulzar on a song for the upcoming film 'Sherdil.The song "Dhoop Paani Bahne De", sung by KK for the film 'Sherdil - The Pilibhit Saga', is the first to be released since his death.
